Hurricanes

Hurricanes are start out as tropical cyclones that form along the Atlantic basin. These tropoical cyclones are classified as hurricanes after they reach a wind speed faster than 74 miles per hour and are further organized as category 1-5 depending on wind speed. The higher category the worse property damage the hurricane can cause.

  1. Hurricane Andrew, 1992: Category 5; hit Miami-Dade county with 165 mile per hour winds and caused estimated 25 billion in property damage.
  2. Hurricane Charley, 2004: Quickly developed into category 4 before landfall on Cayo Costa; 9 people killed and estimated 8.6 billion in property damage.
  3. Hurricane Wilma, 2005: Category 4 hurricane that visciously hit South Florida with wind speed of 100 miles an hour; caused estimated 20.6 billion in property damage but low causalties for a category 4 with only 5 people dead.
  4. Hurricane Irma, 2017: Extensive size and slow moment exacerbated the heavy rainfall and damaging winds; caused estimated 50 billion in property damage, left over 6.5 million Floridians without power, 7 killed by storm directly and another 80 killed indirectly.
  5. Hurricane Micheal, 2018: Made landfall in the panhandle; caused severe storm surges and winds caused catastrophic damage in the Mexico Beach and Panama City Beach areas. 8 were killed directly by the storm, one in Georgia and 7 in Florida and 43 killed inderctly by the storm.
